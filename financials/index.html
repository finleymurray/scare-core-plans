Notes & Assumptions

How We Got Here

AssumptionValueNotes
Scare Core data10 yearsPublished model from Scare Core page (includes scare season + off-season escape rooms, events)
Immersive Core data5 years modelled, 5 years extrapolatedY1–5 from IC page. Y6–10 extrapolated at 5–10% annual growth with maturing margins (37–42%)
Parent overhead£150k (Y1) → £360k (Y10)Group management, accounting, insurance, legal. Scales with operations.
Revenue overlapMinimalScare Core off-season includes escape rooms & events. Old IC theatre (£1M budget) generated modest off-season revenue within the SC model. New IC (£8M) is a completely separate, larger operation. Some overlap exists in Y2–5 (~£200–400k) but is immaterial at group level.
EBITDA multiple8×Conservative for UK entertainment/leisure. Comparables range 6–12× depending on growth and IP value.
Land appreciationNot modelled301 acres of Nottinghamshire farmland with planning permission and developed infrastructure could appreciate significantly. Not included in projections.
IP licensing revenueNot modelledOriginal worlds, characters, and branded experiences could be licensed for touring productions, merchandise, or media. Not included.
InflationNot modelledTicket price increases assume organic growth + reputation building, not inflation adjustment.
