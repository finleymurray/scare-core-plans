Vision & Mission

Not a scream park. A world.

Scare Core exists to prove that horror entertainment can be art. Six interconnected worlds, each with its own mythology, designed from the ground up to create genuinely transformative experiences. No jump-scare corridors. No chainsaw chases. Theatrical storytelling that stays with you.

01

Narrative First

Every maze tells a complete story. Original IP developed in-house, with characters, lore, and interconnected mythology across all six worlds. This isn't a walk-through haunted house — it's immersive theatre in the dark.

02

Year-Round Operation

Scare season runs October to December. The rest of the year, maze barns convert to escape rooms, the Theatre Barn hosts immersive theatre, and the site delivers private events and corporate experiences. Off-season revenue reaches 41% of total turnover by Year 5.

03

Asset, Not Expense

301 acres of freehold land. £7.2M of converted buildings. Proprietary IP. A growing income stream. The £32M investment creates a permanent asset base with a net book value of £23M at Year 10 — not a sunk cost that burns to zero.

The Site

Cowsland Farm, South Leverton

301 acres of isolated Nottinghamshire farmland. Existing agricultural barns converted into six maze structures. 500m separation from nearest residential property. Former Cottam Power Station infrastructure provides grid connections and road access. Within a 2-hour drive of 15 million people.

Operations & Team

Lean Start, Considered Growth

Year 1 operates with 5 permanent staff plus the founder. The team scales to 13 FTEs by Year 6 as off-season programming grows. Seasonal scare performers (130–185) are hired on 12-week contracts. All payroll outsourced to bureau in Years 1–2, brought in-house from Year 3.

Dual Entity Structure

Scare Core Ltd owns the physical attraction — land, buildings, maze infrastructure. Immersive Core Ltd holds the creative IP, technology, and brand assets. Both wholly owned by the founder. This separates operational risk from intellectual property value.

Year 1 Team

CEO/Artistic Director (founder, £0 salary). Managing Director (£95k loaded). Production Manager (£53k). Finance & Admin Manager (£47k). Marketing Manager (£44k). Site Manager (£37k). Total permanent payroll: £276k.

Seasonal Workforce

130–185 performers and support staff on 12-week contracts (October–December). Scare actors at £12–16/hour. Show Captains handle rehearsals and quality control. Full H&S, consent, and character training before season opens.
