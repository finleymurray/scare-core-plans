About the Group

One campus. Three brands. Total creative control.

Core Creative Group is the parent holding company behind a new kind of entertainment business. We own the land, the intellectual property, and the brand rights. Three operating subsidiaries deliver distinct entertainment verticals — each with its own identity, audience, and revenue model — all on one 301-acre campus.

Land & Infrastructure

Core Creative Group holds the freehold on Cowsland Farm — 301 acres of isolated Nottinghamshire farmland with existing agricultural buildings, a farmhouse, and proximity to grid power infrastructure. The land alone is valued at £6.55M and does not depreciate.

Intellectual Property

All original worlds, characters, scripts, and brand assets are owned centrally by Core Creative Group. This IP is licensed to the operating subsidiaries, creating a scalable model where creative work done once generates revenue across multiple channels.

Diversified Revenue

Three brands means three revenue streams across different seasons, audiences, and price points. Scare season runs October to December. Theatre and escape rooms operate year-round. Corporate events, workshops, and venue hire fill the calendar.

Our Brands

Three divisions. One vision.

Each brand operates as its own entity with a distinct identity, audience, and commercial model. Together they form a multi-channel immersive entertainment group with diversified revenue and scalable IP.

The Investment

£32M. 100% Equity. Zero Debt.

Capital is allocated across all four entities: Core Creative Group (land, infrastructure, and site development), Scare Core (attraction build), Immersive Core (theatre), and Escape Core (Year 2 maze retrofits), with a working capital reserve.
