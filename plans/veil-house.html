01 — Concept Overview
A House That Watches Back
A 55m × 38m steel portal frame barn housing a two-storey Victorian mansion with four distinct character wings. Behind every performance room sits a concealed observation laboratory — clinical spaces where the truth of the house is revealed. Eight rooms, eight labs, zero dead space. Every square metre serves the story.

03 — The Four Wings
Cross-Pollination: Exploration I & II
Each wing spans two rooms (one per floor) with a dedicated character pair. In Exploration I, guests explore their assigned wing. In Exploration II (after the character swap), each group is led to a completely different wing — fresh rooms they've never seen, but with a new character whose story directly contradicts what they were told before. No group revisits the same rooms.

Wing A — The Son's Domain

Characters: The Son + The Butler
Ground: Son's Study (writing desk, war memorabilia, fireplace)
First: Son's Bedroom (four-poster, trunk, unfinished letter)
Labs: Lab A1 + Lab A2 (monitor walls, CCTV corkboard, sleep study)
Exploration I: Group A with The Son → Exploration II: Group D (from Medium's wing) arrives with The Assistant

Wing B — The Governess's Domain

Characters: The Governess + The Maid
Ground: Governess's Parlour (piano, sampler, prayer books)
First: Nursery (small beds, rocking horse, music box)
Labs: Lab B1 + Lab B2 (specimen jars, child development files)
Exploration I: Group B with The Governess → Exploration II: Group A (from Son's wing) arrives with The Butler

Wing C — The Housekeeper's Domain

Characters: The Housekeeper + The Gardener
Ground: Housekeeper's Kitchen (range, copper pots, keys)
First: Master Bedroom (vanity, locked jewellery box, medicine)
Labs: Lab C1 + Lab C2 (threshold maps, relationship mapping)
Exploration I: Group C with The Housekeeper → Exploration II: Group B (from Governess's wing) arrives with The Maid

Wing D — The Medium's Domain

Characters: The Medium + The Assistant

05 — Show Structure
The Eight Beats
How 100 guests move through the space across an evening. The cross-pollination model means every group visits two different wings across Explorations I and II, seeing four unique rooms in total. No one revisits the same space.
Beat 1 • 20 min
Opening Ball
All 100 guests in Grand Ballroom. Four characters circulate. Drinks, performance, grouping.
ALL 100
Beat 2 • 25 min
Exploration I
4 groups of 25 explore their assigned wing (2 rooms per wing) with their first character.
4 × 25
Beat 3 • 20 min
The Séance
2 groups of 50 in Séance Chamber (first floor). Two sessions, back to back.
2 × 50
Beat 4 • 20 min
Interval
Bar service. The house transforms behind closed doors. Subtle shifts visible to attentive guests.
RESET
Beat 5 • 15 min
Reconnection
All 100 guests in Dining Room. Characters reunite. Stories start to conflict openly.
ALL 100
Beat 6 • 25 min
Exploration II
Character swap + cross-pollination. Each group explores a DIFFERENT wing with their new character. Contradictions build.
4 × 25 (NEW WINGS)
Beat 7 • 15 min
The Fracture
Groups of ~12 are pulled into their wing's labs via one-way glass. CCTV reveals the truth. Victorian fiction shatters.
8 × ~12 (LABS)
Beat 8 • 15 min
The Reckoning
All 100 in Grand Ballroom. Victorian and lab aesthetics collide. Characters confront their dual identities.
ALL 100
