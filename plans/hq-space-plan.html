Layout Logic

Reception faces north — directly opposite the staff car park and entry gate. Staff tap their NFC badge at the gate, walk straight to reception. Visitors and contractors arrive the same way.

North Wing is the "clean" zone — offices, meetings, creative studio, kitchen, welfare. No industrial processes, no noise, no dust. Comfortable working environment for desk-based staff.

East Wing is the "production" zone — rehearsal, costume, tech, workshop, storage. Noisy, dusty, physical work happens here. The rehearsal hall acts as a buffer between the two zones.

Workshop has its own roller door — 5m wide, facing the service road. Vans and small lorries can reverse directly in. Finished set pieces go straight out without passing through any other room.

Courtyard is compact but functional — reduced from V1 to give the production wing more space. Still has firepit, benches, festoon lights, and the kitchen serving hatch for cast meals.

Flow separation — staff arrive from the north (car park), materials arrive from the south (service road). The two flows never cross. Set pieces move: workshop → storage → loading out via roller door. People move: reception → corridor → wherever they need to be.

V1 → V2 Changes Summary

Workshop: 650 → 3,200 sq ft. Now fits 2 full maze rooms + working clearance. Own roller door, gantry crane, spray booth, compressed air.

Tech department: Didn't exist in V1. Now has a 1,000 sq ft workshop + 800 sq ft dedicated store. Separate from the set build workshop.

Costume department: 650 → 1,100 sq ft studio + 550 sq ft dedicated storage. 10 makeup stations (was 8). Separate humidity-controlled storage.

Reception: Repositioned to face the staff car park. Staff walk straight in from their cars. No more routing around the building.

Rehearsal hall: 1,200 → 1,400 sq ft. Slight expansion.

Courtyard: 4,200 → 1,800 sq ft. Reduced to give production wing the space it needs. Still functional — firepit, benches, serving hatch.

Total footprint: 7,800 → 12,400 sq ft. Budget increased to £6.25m (from £6m) to cover IT equipment and restore contingency to ~8%.

IT equipment: New £45k line covers 15 laptops, MFP copier, initial software licences. Recurring IT costs (M365, Adobe CC, hosting, broadband) sit in the Scare Core annual P&L (~£15k/year).

Build cost per sq ft: ~£215/sq ft (slightly lower than V1 because the workshop is high-volume low-spec space, bringing the average down).
