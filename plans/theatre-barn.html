01 — Concept Overview
A House That Remembers
A purpose-built steel-frame barn on the Scare Core expansion land, housing a complete two-storey Victorian mansion interior with a sunken cellar accessed via real descending staircases. The main floor is raised 1m above the barn slab; the cellar is excavated 1m below it — creating a genuine 2m descent that audiences physically walk down. Every room contains a hidden reveal that transforms between Act Two and Act Four, meaning no two visits through the house are identical.

04 — Hidden Reveals
Secrets of the House
Every room contains a hidden element that is discovered during Act Two (first exploration) and then transforms after the séance in Act Four. Red-bordered cards indicate rooms with physical hidden doors or passages.
Ground Floor
The Library
8m × 6.5m • Ground Floor • 3.0m ceiling
Floor-to-ceiling bookshelves on three walls, leather armchairs, globe, writing desk with correspondence, ladder on brass rail. The son's territory — he spends most of Act Two here, growing increasingly agitated, pulling books from shelves.
A specific book triggers a mechanical latch releasing one shelf section as a concealed pivot door. Behind it: a narrow passage (1.2m wide) leading to a hidden study — desk covered in frantic handwriting, newspaper clippings pinned to walls, a half-finished letter revealing a crucial piece of backstory. Only discovered by the group following the son.
The hidden study lamp is now on. Someone has been in here since Act Two. The half-finished letter has been completed — in different handwriting. A new photograph sits on the desk that wasn't there before.
First Floor
The Nursery
6.5m × 5.5m • Mezzanine Level • 2.8m ceiling
Two small iron beds, a rocking horse, a chest of wooden toys, children's drawings pinned to faded wallpaper. A music box on the windowsill that plays intermittently (automated trigger from show control). The governess's domain.
A jib door — flush with the wallpaper, nearly invisible — swings open on a balanced pivot hinge behind one of the beds. Behind it: a tiny room (2m × 2m), child's handprints on the plaster walls, a small window bricked up from the outside. Scratches around the brickwork as if someone tried to claw their way out. The governess shows this to her group and says the children used to hide here.
The hidden room now has fresh handprints on the walls. Wet. The bricked-up window has a crack that wasn't there before — cold air coming through. The music box is playing continuously and won't stop.
Ground Floor
The Kitchen & Larder
7m × 6.5m • Under Mezzanine • 2.8m ceiling
A working Victorian range (gas effect), copper pans, a long servants' table, meat hooks, drying herbs. The housekeeper's territory. Functional, warm, but with an edge of unease — one chair at the table is always pulled out, as if someone just stood up.
The larder at the back of the kitchen. Shelves of jars, dried herbs, sacks. But the back wall slides aside (motorised, triggered by performer) revealing the top of the cellar stairs. This is how one group accesses the cellar — through a route that feels like it wasn't meant to be found. 12 steep treads descending 2m into the dark.
The larder wall is open. It won't close. Cold air is pouring up from the cellar. The jars on the shelves have shifted — some have fallen and smashed. The chair at the table has moved again.
Ground Floor
The Drawing Room
8m × 6m • Open to Fly Tower • Full height void
The grandest room. Fireplace with mantle, sofas, drinks cabinet (serves real drinks to audience), piano, portrait above the fireplace. The séance table sits centre — round, heavy, with a mechanical rig beneath for movement effects. This room has the full-height void above it for Act Five's spectacle moment.
The portrait above the fireplace is a high-resolution screen behind period glass. During Act Two, the face subtly shifts its gaze to follow specific audience members (tracked by the tech operator). Most people won't consciously notice. The ones who do will feel genuinely unsettled.
During the séance (Act Three): table moves, candles extinguish simultaneously, directional audio voice. In Act Five: the ghost manifests — performer descends on flying rig from the 7m void above, projection mapping transforms the walls, wind and cold effects, full surround sound. The portrait shows a completely different face.
First Floor
The Master Bedroom
7m × 6.5m • Mezzanine Level • 2.8m ceiling
Four-poster bed with heavy drapes, dressing table with brushes and a hand mirror, writing desk with sealed letters, heavy curtains blocking a window that seems to look onto nothing. A large mahogany wardrobe dominates one wall.
The wardrobe is deeper than it should be. Push past the hanging clothes and there's a second compartment — a hidden dressing area with a vanity mirror. But the mirror is a Pepper's Ghost rig (half-silvered glass with a lit cavity behind). At first it reflects normally. Then the tech operator fades in an image behind the glass — a figure standing in the room that isn't there when you turn around.
The Pepper's Ghost is now fully committed. The figure in the mirror is clear, present, breathing. It moves. The vanity mirror on the dressing table outside the wardrobe shows something different to what's in front of it. The sealed letters on the desk have been opened.
Ground Floor
The Parlour
7m × 6.5m • Ground Floor • 3.0m ceiling
A smaller, more intimate sitting room. Two armchairs by a fireplace, a writing desk, a clock that ticks too slowly, family photographs on every surface. This is where the mysterious guest takes their group for quiet, intense 1:1 conversations with individual audience members.
A window on the east wall that has been bricked up from the inside. The mysterious guest draws attention to it — asks the audience why someone would brick up a window from inside a room. During the conversation, if you listen carefully, there's a faint tapping from behind the bricks. Rhythmic. Patient.
The tapping is louder. Persistent. One brick has shifted — light is visible behind it. A different light to anything in the house. The clock has stopped. The photographs on the surfaces have changed — the same faces, but older, or younger, or in different combinations.
Cellar Level
The Cellar
10m × 8m • -1.0m Below Slab • 2.2m ceiling
Every group visits at some point. Accessed via the main cellar stairs (12 treads, genuine 2m descent) or the secret larder stairs. Rough brick walls, flagstone floor, shelving with preserves, old furniture under dust sheets. Oil-lamp lighting. The temperature is noticeably colder. It smells of earth, damp stone, and something faintly sweet.
The bricked-up archway in the far wall. In Act Two, it's sealed — solid brickwork, but with scratched words near the base (a name, a date, a single word: "FORGIVE"). The housekeeper won't go near it. One brick near the top is very slightly loose — you can see mortar dust on the floor beneath it. Something is behind that wall.
Bricks have been removed — from the inside. A gap large enough to see through into a small chamber beyond. A single candle burns in the chamber. A chair faces the opening. There is the clear sense of a presence — sound design, scent (old perfume), a cold spot from directional air. For one group, a performer is physically present in the chamber. For others, it's empty but the candle is still lit.
First Floor
The Locked Room
3m × 4.5m • Mezzanine Level • Magnetically locked
Locked during Act Two — audience can see the door but it won't open. The mysterious guest has the key. In Act Four, one group is led inside. This is the narrative climax: the room where everything happened. Small, sparse, claustrophobic. A single bed, a chair, a window with bars. The truth of the house is written on the walls.
The room itself is the reveal. It doesn't exist during Act Two — the door is locked, the room is just a rumour. When it finally opens in Act Four, the audience enters the emotional core of the story. What they find depends on the narrative, but architecturally: the walls are covered in layered wallpaper that's been torn back to reveal writing beneath, the window looks out onto a back-projected image that changes, and the floor creaks in a pattern that mirrors footsteps from the cellar below.
05 — Show Structure
Two-Hour Narrative Arc
The performance alternates between together moments (full cast, all audience) and split moments (4 groups of ~50, following different characters through different rooms). Every group visits the cellar at a different point. The show is controlled from the technical gallery via QLab show control.
0:00 — 0:30
Act I: The Gathering
All 200 guests together. Drinks reception in the drawing room. Meet the household. Slow build. Then something shifts.
Together • 200 guests
0:30 — 1:10
Act II: The Exploration
Audience splits into 4 groups of ~50. Each follows a character. Each discovers a secret. Every group visits the cellar at a different time. Intimate moments.
Split • 4 × 50
1:10 — 1:30
Act III: The Séance
All reconvene in the drawing room. The séance. Table moves. Candles die. A voice answers. One character vanishes.
Together • 200 guests
1:30 — 1:45
Interval
Exit to The Yard. Drinks, compare notes, decompress. The house resets for Act IV behind closed doors.
The Yard • 15 min
1:45 — 2:15
Act IV: The Search
Back inside. House has changed. New groups, new routes. The secrets have evolved. Cellar arch is breached. Locked room opens. The truth emerges.
Split • 4 × 50
2:15 — 2:30
Act V: The Haunting
Full spectacle. Ghost manifests via flying rig in the drawing room void. Projection, wind, sound, cold. Then resolution. Exit one by one through a final corridor for a personal last moment.
