Overview

Introduction

This document defines the visual identity, tone, and usage rules for Core Creative Group and all subsidiary and experience brands.

Purpose
These guidelines exist to ensure consistency across every touchpoint — from digital marketing and printed materials to on-site signage and merchandise. Every member of the team, every agency partner, and every supplier should use this document as the single source of truth for how our brands look, sound, and feel.

Structure

Brand Architecture

Core Creative Group operates a branded house model. The parent company owns all IP and creative direction, with three operating subsidiaries each serving a distinct entertainment vertical.

Hierarchy
The CCG icon and wordmark should never appear on public-facing guest materials. It exists purely as the corporate and investor-facing identity. Guests interact with the subsidiary brands and their experience brands.

Brand Visibility Rules
Guest-Facing

Use subsidiary logos (Scare Core, Immersive Core, Escape Core) and experience logos on all customer touchpoints: tickets, signage, social media, advertising, and merchandise.

Corporate / Investor

Use the Core Creative Group logo and identity on business plans, investor materials, legal documents, supplier contracts, and internal communications.

Never

Never place the CCG parent logo on guest-facing materials. The corporate brand should be invisible to the public. Guests visit "Scare Core", not "a Core Creative Group experience".

Immersive Core

The Haunting of Veil House

A two-hour immersive theatre experience set across 22 rooms of a decaying Victorian mansion. Audiences split into four groups and move through the house, uncovering the story of what happened within its walls.

Show Overview
The Haunting of Veil House is Core Creative Group's flagship Immersive Core production. It occupies the full Theatre Barn venue — three levels, 22 rooms, 12,000 sq ft of performance space. Up to 200 guests per performance, running approximately two hours.

Show Structure
The production runs across five acts. Audiences begin together in the Grand Hall, then split into four groups of approximately 50 who take different paths through the house before reuniting for the finale.
Act I

The Arrival

All 200 guests gather in the Grand Hall. The host welcomes them. The house begins to reveal itself.

Act II

The Separation

Groups split. Each follows a different character through different wings of the house.

Act III

The Descent

Groups move deeper into the house. The cellar level. Hidden passages. The truth begins to surface.

Act IV

The Transformation

The house changes. Rooms transform. What was familiar becomes wrong. The veil lifts.

Act V

The Reckoning

All groups reunite. The final confrontation. The house decides who leaves and who stays.

Brand Rules
Do

Use the gold/black/cream palette consistently across all Veil House materials. The warmth of candlelight and aged surfaces is central to the identity.

Do

Use close-up, detail-focused imagery. Tease textures, objects, and atmosphere rather than showing full rooms or environments.

Do

Present the logo on dark backgrounds only — Mansion Black (#1C1810) or pure black. The gold texture needs contrast to read correctly.

Don't

Never recreate the logo in flat colour, outline, or plain typography. It is a fixed textured artwork and must always be used from the supplied files.

Don't

Never show full room layouts or floor plans in public-facing materials. The experience depends on discovery — keep the interior a mystery.

Don't

Never use the Deep Crimson as a primary colour. It is an accent only — blood, danger, the supernatural. Overuse cheapens the effect.

Standards

Tone of Voice

Each brand speaks differently to its audience, but all share a foundation of confidence, authenticity, and directness.

Brand Voices

Core Creative Group

Professional, measured, and authoritative. Speaks to investors, partners, and industry. Data-driven and confident without being arrogant. Never uses superlatives without evidence to back them up.

"A £32M immersive entertainment investment built on 301 acres of freehold land, generating projected returns across three distinct entertainment verticals."

Scare Core

Bold, provocative, and darkly playful. Speaks directly to thrill-seekers. Short, punchy sentences. Uses tension and mystery. Never explains too much — let the experience speak.

"Six worlds. One night. No mercy. This isn't a theme park. This is survival."

Immersive Core

Elegant, atmospheric, and slightly cryptic. Speaks to theatre-goers and experience seekers. Evocative language that creates imagery. More poetic than the other brands.

"Step through the threshold. Leave the world you know at the door. Tonight, the story needs you."

Escape Core

Clever, energetic, and challenging. Speaks to puzzle enthusiasts and groups. Uses questions and teasers. Implies intelligence and rewards curiosity.

"Think you're clever? Prove it. 60 minutes. One locked room. Every detail matters."
Universal Principles
Always

Be direct and confident. Write like a human, not a press release. Let the experiences sell themselves. Use active voice. Be specific over vague.

Never

Use corporate buzzwords, clichés, or filler. Don't over-explain experiences — mystery is the product. Avoid passive voice and weak qualifiers like "quite" or "rather".

Standards

Usage Rules

Protecting the integrity of every brand mark across all applications.

Logo Clear Space
All logos require a minimum clear space equal to the height of the "C" in "CORE" on all sides. No other elements, text, or graphics should encroach on this space.
Do's and Don'ts
Do

Always use logos from the approved master files. Maintain the proportions exactly as provided. Use approved colour variants only.

Do

Ensure sufficient contrast between the logo and its background. Test at small sizes to ensure legibility.

Do

Use the CCG icon mark at small sizes where the full wordmark would be illegible (favicons, app icons, social profile pictures).

Don't

Never stretch, squash, rotate, or distort any logo. Never change the colours outside of the approved variants.

Don't

Never place logos on busy photographic backgrounds without a solid or semi-transparent backing panel.

Don't

Never recreate the maze pattern in Escape Core, the shattered E in Scare Core, or any maze world logo texture. Always use master artwork files.

Don't

Never add effects (drop shadows, outer glows, bevels, outlines) to any logo unless specified in the master artwork.

Don't

Never combine subsidiary logos with the CCG parent logo on the same material. They serve different audiences and contexts.

Minimum Sizes
To ensure legibility and brand integrity across all applications.
Digital

Full wordmarks: minimum 120px wide. Icon marks: minimum 32px. Favicons: 16px (icon only).

Print

Full wordmarks: minimum 30mm wide. Icon marks: minimum 10mm. Maze logos: minimum 40mm wide to preserve texture detail.

File Formats
Master logo files are available in the following formats. Always use the highest quality format available for the application.
For Print

PNG (high-res, transparent background). Request vector SVG/AI files from the creative team for large-format printing.

For Digital

WebP for web use (smaller file size). PNG for social media and presentations. SVG for responsive web elements.
